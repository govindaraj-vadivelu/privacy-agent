# Overview

Real-world use cases for an **Agentic Privacy Assistant**:

---

## 1. Personal Privacy Audit

**User:** "I want to know what data Google, Facebook, and Amazon have on me."

**Agent:**

- Fetches privacy policies for all three
- Identifies what data each collects (location, browsing, purchases)
- Compares risk levels
- Drafts data export requests (GDPR Article 15)
- Submits requests on user's behalf (with approval)

**Outcome:** User gets a full report + submitted requests in 5 minutes instead of hours of reading.

---

## 2. App Vetting for Parents

**User:** "My kid wants to install TikTok. Is it safe?"

**Agent:**

- Fetches TikTok's privacy policy
- Checks for: data sharing with third parties, age restrictions, location tracking
- Flags: "TikTok shares data with advertisers and tracks location. Minimum age: 13."
- Suggests alternatives with better privacy scores

**Outcome:** Informed decision in seconds.

---

## 3. GDPR Compliance for Small Businesses

**User (business owner):** "I need to make sure my website is GDPR compliant."

**Agent:**

- Analyzes their current privacy policy
- Compares against GDPR requirements (right to erasure, data portability, consent)
- Flags missing sections: "You don't mention how long you store user data."
- Drafts updated policy text

**Outcome:** Compliance check without hiring a lawyer.

---

## 4. Policy Change Monitoring

**User:** "Track the privacy policies of all my subscriptions."

**Agent:**

- Monitors Netflix, Spotify, Dropbox, etc.
- Detects when a policy changes
- Alerts: "Spotify updated their data sharing policy — now shares listening history with marketing partners."
- Drafts opt-out request if possible

**Outcome:** Proactive privacy protection.

---

## 5. Job Seeker Due Diligence

**User:** "I'm interviewing at Company X. What's their data privacy culture like?"

**Agent:**

- Fetches Company X's privacy policy
- Checks for employee data clauses (monitoring, surveillance)
- Searches for data breach history
- Summarizes: "Company X uses keystroke monitoring software. 2 data breaches in the past 3 years."

**Outcome:** Red flags before signing an offer.

---

## 6. Browser Extension for Real-Time Warnings

**User:** Browsing a random website.

**Agent (as extension):**

- Detects when user visits a new site
- Fetches privacy policy in the background
- Shows a popup: "⚠️ This site shares your data with 47 third parties."
- Offers to block trackers or opt out

**Outcome:** Privacy awareness at browsing time, not after the fact.

---

## 7. Legal Aid for Low-Income Users

**User:** "I got a notice that my data was leaked. What are my rights?"

**Agent:**

- Identifies user's location (determines GDPR, CCPA, or other laws)
- Explains rights: "You can demand compensation. Here's how."
- Drafts a complaint letter
- Finds pro-bono legal resources

**Outcome:** Access to justice without expensive lawyers.

---

## 8. IoT Device Safety Check

**User:** "I bought a smart doorbell. Should I trust it?"

**Agent:**

- Fetches manufacturer's privacy policy
- Checks for: cloud storage, third-party access, camera footage sharing
- Flags: "Ring shares footage with law enforcement without a warrant in some cases."
- Recommends privacy-focused alternatives

**Outcome:** Informed purchase decisions.

---

## 9. Dating App Safety

**User:** "I'm using Tinder. What data are they collecting?"

**Agent:**

- Analyzes Tinder's privacy policy
- Flags: "Tinder shares your location, messages, and swipe history with Match Group and advertisers."
- Suggests: "Turn off precise location. Use a burner email."

**Outcome:** Safer online dating.

---

## 10. Activist/Journalist Threat Modeling

**User (journalist):** "I need to use Signal, Proton Mail, and Tor. Are they really private?"

**Agent:**

- Audits privacy policies of all three
- Cross-references with third-party security audits
- Confirms: "Signal is end-to-end encrypted and doesn't log metadata. ProtonMail logs IP addresses (use VPN)."
- Recommends OPSEC improvements

**Outcome:** Threat-informed privacy stack.

---

## Why These Use Cases Work with Agentic AI

**Traditional tools** require users to:

- Find the privacy policy
- Read it (average: 15 pages, 30 minutes)
- Understand legal jargon
- Take action (if they remember)

**Agentic AI:**

- Does all of this autonomously
- Explains in plain language
- Acts on the user's behalf (with consent)

*It's not just a tool—it's a privacy advocate in your pocket.*
